Where to sell Fish and Meat in Sea of Thieves

Distinguishing from the majority of factions in Sea of Thieves, the Hunter's Call is unique in terms of turning in cooked fish. Rather than delivering your freshly prepared catches to any standard outpost, you must seek out the special seaposts. These small piers perched on rocks can be found scattered across the vast map of Sea of Thieves. At these specific locations, you can trade in your delectable cooked fish for rewards and reputation with the Hunter's Call faction. These seaposts provide a quaint and convenient way for pirates to exchange their culinary delights and partake in the culinary expertise of the sea's bounty.

The following is a list of every Seapost and their map coordinates: *Hover over a Seapost Name to display its location

  • Brian’s Bazaar Y12
  • The finest trading Post F17
  • The North Star Seapost H10
  • Roaring traders U20
  • The Spoils of Plenty Store B7
  • Stephen’s Spoils L15
  • Three Paces East Seapost S10
  • The Wild Treasures Store O4

At each of these seaposts you can find a representative of the Hunter's Call, the faction that you can sell your fish to. They're normally standing near the end of the pier, not in the shop.

What can you sell

Maximizing the value of your catches is essential when selling fish to the Hunter's Call. Cooked fish is particularly lucrative, making it a wise decision to utilize the onboard pan on your ship for cooking before turning them in. However, caution must be exercised, as burnt fish is the least valuable type, even less so than raw fish. During the cooking process, it's crucial to monitor the fish closely. Watch for the eyes to turn white, as this signifies that the fish is perfectly cooked and ready to be removed from the pan. Cooking the fish any longer will lead to burning, diminishing its value, while removing it prematurely will result in undercooked fish. By mastering the art of cooking fish to perfection, you can ensure the highest profits when selling them to the Hunter's Call faction at the seaposts. Remember, a well-cooked fish is the key to reaping the most rewards for your efforts on the high seas.

The price of each fish / meat is dependant one their Rarity, Type and State of the fish when you sell it.

Baits in Sea of Thieves

  • Leeches seem drawn to the water. They are best found by digging as close to the water line as possible.

  • Earthworms tend to nestle themselves beneath the grass.

  • Grubs appear to enjoy the warm sand. Just be sure you’re at least little further back from where you might find leeches.

There is also a possibility of obtaining bait from barrels that can be found on the main islands as well as the smaller ones, and also in crates.

Regions for Fishing in Sea of Thieves

Shipwrecks are sunken ships that can contain various Resources and Treasure. They are the only locations where players can fish for Wreckers. *A large flock of Seagulls flying above an area at sea indicates a shipwreck is below

A Storm is an always present dark cloud roaming the seas which can be easily spotted from afar. It contains heavy rain and wind, wild waves, and dangerous lightning ponds

FISHING TUTORIAL (for beginners)

How To Fish 1. Decide your bait. Pull out your fishing rod, re-open your inventory and when holding"R"(or B on controller), decide on the bait you want. Once it's on the end of your hook, you're ready to cast. 2. To cast, simply point at a body of water, and left click. To retrieve your line, simply right click. 3. Once your line is in the water, it's simply a case of waiting. Sometimes you'll be able to see a fish under the water, others it'll be too dark. Either way, once you've got a bite your line will begin to flex and bow. 4. To reel in a fish when you have a bite, don't instantly left click (it'll cause the line to break and the fish to escape). What you need to do is tire the fish out. Fundamentally, you move your fishing rod in the opposite direction that the fish is moving. If the fish is going left, hold right (typically "D"). If the fish is going right, hold left (typically "A"). If the fish is going away from you, hold down (typically "S"). !!!If you're holding the rod in the wrong direction, you'll hear it begin to strain and crack. When your line goes slack and when the fish is exhausted, reel it in.!!! 5. To take your fish off your line, you need to be sure you have the inventory space. If you don't have space, you can store items in a nearby barrel or eat some existing food.

How To Cook It's fairly simple! Head below deck and you'll see a stove and skillet. If you aren't on board your ship, most islands have an available frying pan by any fire. With your fish in hand, you'll be able to interact with the pan, and start cooking. Like all things in Sea of Thieves, there's no UI to indicate how well your fish is cooked. You'll need to pay attention. If it's undercooked and you eat it, you'll be sick with food poisoning. Left on the heat too long, and it'll be burnt and worthless. While it takes practice to know exactly which fish have which timings, the easiest tricks are: Watch for the fish to change colour. When it turns brown, it's typically cooked just right. The fish's pupils will go completely white. Steam coming off the frying pan will thin out as the fish cooks through. Larger fish (trophies) take longer to cook.
