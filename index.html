Fishing And Cooking Guide

Where to sell Fish and Meat in Sea of Thieves

Distinguishing from the majority of factions in Sea of Thieves, the Hunter's Call is unique in terms of turning in cooked fish. Rather than delivering your freshly prepared catches to any standard outpost, you must seek out the special seaposts. These small piers perched on rocks can be found scattered across the vast map of Sea of Thieves. At these specific locations, you can trade in your delectable cooked fish for rewards and reputation with the Hunter's Call faction. These seaposts provide a quaint and convenient way for pirates to exchange their culinary delights and partake in the culinary expertise of the sea's bounty.

The following is a list of every Seapost and their map coordinates: *Hover over a Seapost Name to display its location

  • Brian’s Bazaar Y12
  • The finest trading Post F17
  • The North Star Seapost H10
  • Roaring traders U20
  • The Spoils of Plenty Store B7
  • Stephen’s Spoils L15
  • Three Paces East Seapost S10
  • The Wild Treasures Store O4

At each of these seaposts you can find a representative of the Hunter's Call, the faction that you can sell your fish to. They're normally standing near the end of the pier, not in the shop.

What can you sell

Maximizing the value of your catches is essential when selling fish to the Hunter's Call. Cooked fish is particularly lucrative, making it a wise decision to utilize the onboard pan on your ship for cooking before turning them in. However, caution must be exercised, as burnt fish is the least valuable type, even less so than raw fish. During the cooking process, it's crucial to monitor the fish closely. Watch for the eyes to turn white, as this signifies that the fish is perfectly cooked and ready to be removed from the pan. Cooking the fish any longer will lead to burning, diminishing its value, while removing it prematurely will result in undercooked fish. By mastering the art of cooking fish to perfection, you can ensure the highest profits when selling them to the Hunter's Call faction at the seaposts. Remember, a well-cooked fish is the key to reaping the most rewards for your efforts on the high seas.

To sum up, you can sell on Seapost:

The price of each fish / meat is dependant one their Rarity, Type and State of the fish when you sell it.

*Half eaten fish Cannot be Sold, so be careful if your inventory is full of fish.

How to find Baits for Fishing in Sea of Thieves

Leeches

Leeches seem drawn to the water. They are best found by digging as close to the water line as possible.

Earthworms

Earthworms tend to nestle themselves beneath the grass.

Grubs

Grubs appear to enjoy the warm sand. Just be sure you’re at least little further back from where you might find leeches.

There is also a possibility of obtaining bait from barrels that can be found on the main islands as well as the smaller ones, and also in crates.

Regions for Fishing in Sea of Thieves


*Click on the Map to find out More

The Ancient Isles

The Ancient Isles is one of the main Regions in The Sea of Thieves located on the south-western region of the World Map. The Ancient Isles is mostly a bright and sunny place with dark blue waters and thick vegetation among large rock monuments where you can catch Ancientscales.

Shores of Plenty

Shores of Plenty is located on the North-Western region of the World map. The Shores of Plenty's inviting bright and sunny environment induces a more calming aroma to wandering pirates. Here, the climate is tropical with the water a clear light blue, the sands Silver-white with Palm-filled Shores and lush vegetation. In the waters of the Shores of Plenty you can catch Plentifins

The Wilds

The Wilds is located on the Northeastern region of the World Map. It is a harsh and miserable environment with green-yellowish seas and constantly overcast clouds, giving it a dark and gloomy atmosphere. In the open waters of The Wilds you can catch Wildsplashes.

The Devil's Roar

The Devil's Roar is a region in the south-eastern Sea of Thieves characterized by its harsh, volcanic environment. The region is composed of a mixture of small and large rocky Islands usually formed around the region's numerous Volcanoes, covered with scorched plant life and red crystalline growths where you can catch Devilfish in the open waters.

Shipwrecks

Shipwrecks are sunken ships that can contain various Resources and Treasure. They are the only locations where players can fish for Wreckers.
*A large flock of Seagulls flying above an area at sea indicates that a shipwreck is below them.

Storms

Storm is an always present dark cloud roaming the seas which can be easily spotted from afar. If it contains heavy rain and wind, wild waves, and dangerous lightning so then you know that you can catch Stormfish. Remember that Storms do not go to The Devil's Roar Region so if you want to catch some Stormfishes, there is no point waiting there for Storm.
*Storms are also indicated by the ringing of the Ship's Bell

Ponds

Ponds are a feature of many islands scattered across the Sea of Thieves. They are the only source of freshwater and the only locations where the Pondie can be caught.

Skeleton Ships

Skeleton Ships can be found as patrol ships peacefully sailing between islands or through emergent encounters.

Skeleton Fort

Skeleton Fort is one of the possible World Events in Sea of Thieves, indicated by a large gray Cloud in the shape of a Skull. An active Skeleton Fort can appear at any Fortress Island *apart from Fort of the Damned, which has its own Raid event

The Fort of Fortune

The Fort of Fortune is a rare type of World Event. Its structure is similar to the current Skeleton Fort World Event but provides a greater challenge and holds a greater reward.The Fort of Fortune’s Skull Cloud differs from the standard Skeleton Fort Skull Cloud with its glowing red scars and eyes.

Fort of the Damned

Fort of the Damned is a Fortress Raid located in The Ancient Isles L-14 . Unlike other World Events, the Fort of the Damned Raid is manually activated by players. Once the Fort is active, the Skull will appear above Fort of the Damned so be careful.

TIP #5

Hidden Fishing Spot with Battlegills
*Only for Pirate Legend

If you want to Farm Battlegills, there is a spot where no one will bother you, and you can easily sell your fish to earn some easy money.

  1. First, make sure that a Fleet Cloud World Event is active on the server.
  2. Next, go to the Pirate Legend entrance, located at every tavern, and play the tune.
  3. Pass through the waterfall and emerge on the other side. The Pirate Legend Hideout should be located underneath the Fleet Cloud World Event, meaning that if the cloud is active, you can fish for battlegills there.
  4. Start fishing, and you will see results. Every type of battlegill can be caught here, including Trophy and Night-time variants.
  5. If you reach level 100 in Guardians of Fortune, you can sell your fish directly to ghost Merrick without having to resurface.

Pirate Legend Title can be given to players that have reached level 50 in three different Trading Companies, and bought the corresponding level 50 promotions. Players must then speak to the Mysterious Stranger, who will reward the Title, and the Shanty of Legends that's used to access the Athena's Fortune Hideout.

TIP #6

Optimal Path for Gathering Fish and Meat
*In My Opinion

If you're looking to make the most out of fishing and hunting, I highly recommend targeting Wreckers and taking over forts. Here's how you can do it:

  1. First things first, prepare your bait. Stock up on Earthworms.
  2. Sail out and look for a Shipwreck. Once you find one, loot all the food, meat, and fish. Remember, you can cook and sell everything, even if it's burnt.
  3. Fish near the shipwreck to attract Wreckers. Your goal should be to catch Trophy Wreckers and Snow Wreckers. If you're fishing during a storm, you may even catch Blackcloud Wreckers.
  4. If you run out of bait or feel like doing something else, head to the nearest Fort. Clear it of ghosts and make sure the area is safe.
  5. Collect meat and fish from the fort barrels. Cook them on Four Pans to save time. Use a timer to prevent burning.
    Note: The meat and fish in the barrels may already be partially cooked, so keep an eye on them!
  6. While cooking, you can also gather loot from the fort and load it onto your ship. If you have extra time, you can continue fishing near the fort.
  7. When you're done, sail to the nearest SeaPost to sell your cooked fish and meat. Then head to a Outposts to sell your fort loot.

This is just my personal strategy for fishing and earning. Feel free to tweak it or skip steps—it's your adventure, after all. The most important thing is to have fun!

The cherry on top would be to sell all your treasures during a Gold Rush event. This will allow you to take advantage of the 1.5x reward multiplier, maximizing your earnings.

FISHING TUTORIAL
(for beginners)

How to fish in Sea of Thieves

  1. Decide your bait. Pull out your fishing rod, re-open your inventory and when holding "R"(or B on controller), decide on the bait you want. Once it's on the end of your hook, you're ready to cast.
  2. To cast, simply point at a body of water, and left click. To retrieve your line, simply right click.
  3. Once your line is in the water, it's simply a case of waiting. Sometimes you'll be able to see a fish under the water, others it'll be too dark. Either way, once you've got a bite your line will begin to flex and bow.
  4. To reel in a fish when you have a bite, don't instantly left click (it'll cause the line to break and the fish to escape). What you need to do is tire the fish out. Fundamentally, you move your fishing rod in the opposite direction that the fish is moving. If the fish is going left, hold right (typically "D"). If the fish is going right, hold left (typically "A"). If the fish is going away from you, hold down (typically "S").

    Note: If you're holding the rod in the wrong direction, you'll hear it begin to strain and crack. When your line goes slack and when the fish is exhausted, reel it in.
  5. To take your fish off your line, you need to be sure you have the inventory space. If you don't have space, you can store items in a nearby barrel or eat some existing food.

How to cook fish in Sea of Thieves

  1. It's fairly simple! Head below deck and you'll see a stove and skillet. If you aren't on board your ship, most islands have an available frying pan by any fire.
  2. With your fish in hand, you'll be able to interact with the pan, and start cooking.
  3. Like all things in Sea of Thieves, there's no UI to indicate how well your fish is cooked. You'll need to pay attention. If it's undercooked and you eat it, you'll be sick with food poisoning. Left on the heat too long, and it'll be burnt and worthless.
  4. While it takes practice to know exactly which fish have which timings, the easiest tricks are:
    • Steam coming off the frying pan will thin out as the fish cooks through.
    • Watch for the fish to change colour. When it turns brown, it's typically cooked just right.

For easier fish cooking, consider using the Cook Timer.
